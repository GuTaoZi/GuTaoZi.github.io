Lecture 1 - Introduction

Three Pillars of Computer Security

  • Confidentiality involves the protection of computer systems and data privacy, preventing unauthorized access or disclosure.
  • Integrity concerns the protection of computer systems and data from unauthorized alteration or corruption.
  • Availability ensures that computer systems and networks are operational, unaffected by accidental failures or malicious attacks.

Three More Concepts in Computer Security

  • Authenticity verifies the identity of a subject or a resource.
  • Accountability assures that actions performed can be traced and recorded.
  • Non-repudiation prevents entities from denying their actions or occurrences of events.

The 3A Principle in Computer Security

  • Authentication recognizes and records who accesses information.
  • Authorization gives specific permissions to users based on actual demands.
  • Accounting collects and traces interactions between users and the system.

Proving Methods in Computer Security

  • Formal Verification
  • Security Proofs
  • Logical Measures
